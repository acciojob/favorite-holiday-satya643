Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a stunning city known for its classical Buddhist temples, gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It offers a peaceful and cultural escape into Japan's rich heritage.

  1. Fushimi Inari Shrine – Famous for its thousands of vermillion torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) – A dazzling golden temple surrounded by water.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove – A serene walk through towering bamboo stalks.
  4. Kiyomizu-dera Temple – A historic temple with a breathtaking wooden stage.
  5. Gion District – A famous geisha area with traditional teahouses.
  • Sushi – Fresh and beautifully crafted bites of rice and fish.
  • Yudofu – A delicate tofu hot pot found near Zen temples.
  • Kaiseki – A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Matcha Desserts – Green tea flavored sweets and pastries.
  • Tempura – Lightly battered and fried vegetables or seafood.
